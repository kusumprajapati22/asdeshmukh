Indian Economic Outlook Post-Budget 2024: What Can We Expect?

Uploaded On: 02 Jul 2024 Author: Knowledge Management Team Like (180) Comment (0)

Introduction
India's 2024 budget declaration has led to stakeholders in different sectors evaluating how the country's economic landscape may be affected by it. Being a top CA firm, we have analyzed various elements of the budget so as to give an overall picture of what can be expected in the next year by businesses, investors and the public at large.

Projections for economic growth
In order to drive economic growth, the 2024 budget is focused on making huge investments in infrastructure, technology and rural development. The government’s efforts towards improving connectivity as well as modernizing farming methods will most likely lead to higher GDP growth rates. Expectations are that there will be strong growth rates between 6% and 7% with FDI inflows increasing and the manufacturing sector being revived through the ‘Make in India’ campaign.

Tax reforms and their effects
Among this year’s most notable budget features is tax reform. Simplified tax slabs have been introduced while personal income tax deductions were raised aiming at boosting consumer spending power. Businesses especially small and medium enterprises (SMEs) will benefit from reduced corporate taxes thereby encouraging plough-back of profits into expansionary activities.

Personal Tax: Increase in tax exemption limits and deductions for health insurance and education loans.

Corporate Tax: Reduction in rates for small to medium enterprises to foster growth and employment.

These changes not only promise to enhance disposable incomes but also reflect a move towards a more progressive tax system.

Sector-Specific Highlights

Technology and Startups: The budget allocates funds for technology incubators and startup hubs, which will drive innovation and support the burgeoning startup ecosystem in India.

Infrastructure: An increased outlay for infrastructure development, especially in transport and renewable energy, indicates the government's push towards sustainable development. 

Agriculture: Enhanced support for organic farming and modern agricultural practices is set to increase the sector's productivity and farmers' incomes.

These targeted measures are designed to create job opportunities, promote sustainable practices, and ensure inclusive growth across all regions of the country.

Fiscal Management and Inflation
Fiscal prudence has been a cornerstone of this budget, with a clear roadmap for reducing the fiscal deficit. The government's strategy to manage public expenditure and boost revenue through divestments and digital innovations in tax collections is expected to stabilize economic fundamentals. This, in turn, is likely to keep inflation within the targeted range, thus preserving purchasing power and ensuring economic stability.

Conclusion
The 2024 budget presents a balanced blend of growth stimulation, sector-specific interventions, and fiscal prudence. As a CA firm with deep expertise and a commitment to trust and transparency, we believe that these measures will pave the way for a resilient and prosperous economic future for India.

For businesses, it's a signal to gear up for new opportunities, and for individuals, it's a chance to benefit from a stronger economy. As always, we are here to assist our clients in navigating these changes effectively, ensuring that they are well-positioned to thrive in the evolving economic landscape.
