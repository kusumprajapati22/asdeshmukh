Beyond Profit: Why ESG Matters Now More Than Ever

Uploaded On: 04 Feb 2025 Author: Knowledge Management Team Like (29) Comment (0)

In today's ever-evolving business landscape, success is no longer measured by financial performance. Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors have emerged as critical factors indicating a company's long-term success. Investors, regulators, and consumers are increasingly evaluating businesses through the lens of ESG, making it imperative for organisations to align their operations with ESG principles.

Understanding ESG
ESG represents a broad framework, consisting of three main pillars:

● Environmental: The environmental aspect of the ESG framework focuses on how a company impacts and manages its environmental risks, including carbon emissions, resource consumption, and waste management.

● Social: The organisation’s relationship with employees, communities, and society at large, comes under the social aspect of the ESG framework. This segment covers aspects such as diversity and corporate social responsibility.

● Governance: The internal systems and policies ensuring ethical decision-making, transparency, and accountability of the business are scrutinised under the governance sector of the ESG framework.

The Growing Importance of ESG in India
India is witnessing a paradigm shift in corporate responsibility. The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has mandated Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR) for the top 1,000 listed companies, underscoring the need for ESG compliance. 

Additionally, investors are increasingly prioritising ESG-driven companies, recognising that sustainable businesses are more resilient and capable of generating long-term value.

5 Reasons Why ESG Matters Now More Than Ever

1. Investor Confidence and Access to Capital
Institutional investors and global funds now incorporate ESG metrics into their investment decisions. Companies with strong ESG credentials are more likely to attract investment, secure lower-cost capital, and build investor trust.

2. Regulatory Compliance and Risk Management
Governments worldwide, including India, are tightening regulations around environmental protection and corporate governance. Non-compliance with the ESG regulations can lead to penalties, reputational damage, and legal repercussions. ESG-focused businesses, thus get an upper hand in mitigating these risks by adhering to the evolving regulations.

3. Competitive Advantage and Brand Reputation
Consumers today are more conscious of the ethical and environmental footprint of businesses they support. Companies that champion sustainability, diversity, and ethical governance enjoy increased brand loyalty, stronger customer relationships, and a competitive edge in the marketplace.

4. Operational Efficiency and Cost Savings 
Sustainable practices, such as energy efficiency, waste reduction, and responsible supply chain management, contribute to cost savings and operational efficiency of businesses. Thus, businesses that integrate ESG principles experience reduced burden of expenses and improved optimisation of resources.

5. Talent Attraction and Employee Satisfaction
A strong ESG framework fosters an inclusive, purpose-driven workplace culture. Employees are more inclined to work for organisations that prioritise social responsibility, diversity, and ethical governance, leading to higher retention rates and improved productivity. This ultimately impacts the overall business growth and success rate, making ESG one of the most crucial factors for comprehensive growth.

The Role of Chartered Accountants in ESG Compliance

Chartered accountants play a crucial role in guiding businesses on ESG compliance, sustainability reporting, and risk assessment. Leading from the front, chartered accountants assist in measuring ESG performance, integrating sustainable financial strategies, and ensuring adherence to regulatory requirements.

With the capability to measure and report on ESG performance, evaluate sustainability-related risks and opportunities and incorporate these insights into financial reporting and strategy, the expertise of chartered accountants plays a crucial role in transitioning businesses to a sustainable economy.

Conclusion:
With increasing global and domestic emphasis on sustainability, ESG has become an imperative aspect of any and every business. Companies that embed ESG into their core strategy are better positioned to thrive in the future, attracting investors, customers, and top talent while making a positive impact on society.
